FAQ helper notes

- If the answer is not in the knowledge base, say so clearly.

Q: What do I do if I miss a dose of my medicine?
A: Take it as soon as you remember. But if it is almost time for your next dose, just skip the missed one. Never take two doses at once.

Q: How do I know if my blood pressure is normal?
A: A normal reading is below 120 over 80. If it is above 130 over 80 on two different readings, tell your doctor. Always write down your readings to share at your next visit.

Q: What are the signs of low blood sugar (for diabetics)?
A: You may feel shaky, sweaty, confused, very hungry, or have a headache. Drink a small glass of fruit juice or eat a few sweets right away. Then rest and tell your family. If it does not get better in 15 minutes, call 119.

Q: Can I take my medicines with coffee or tea?
A: It is always safest to take medicine with a plain glass of water. Some medicines are affected by coffee, tea, or milk. Ask your doctor or pharmacist if you are unsure.

Q: What if I feel a new side effect after taking medicine?
A: Write it down — what happened, when, and which medicine. Tell your doctor at your next visit. If the side effect feels serious (e.g. trouble breathing, chest pain, severe rash), call 119 immediately.

Q: How should I store my medicines?
A: Keep medicines in a cool, dry place away from sunlight. Do not store them in the bathroom (too humid) or kitchen (too hot near the stove). Keep them away from children.

Q: Is it safe to eat grapefruit with my medicines?
A: Grapefruit can affect how some medicines work, especially cholesterol medicines like Atorvastatin. If in doubt, avoid grapefruit and ask your doctor or pharmacist.

Q: I feel dizzy when I stand up. Is that normal?
A: Feeling dizzy when you stand up quickly is called postural hypotension. It can be a side effect of blood pressure medicine. Stand up slowly, hold on to something, and wait a few seconds before walking. Tell your doctor if it happens often.

Q: How much water should I drink each day?
A: Most adults need 6 to 8 glasses of water per day. Drink more if it is hot or if you exercise. Avoid too much sweet drinks or soda.

Q: When should I call emergency (119)?
A: Call 119 immediately if you or someone nearby has:
- Chest pain or tightness
- Trouble breathing
- Sudden weakness or numbness on one side
- Sudden confusion or trouble speaking
- A bad fall with possible injury
Do not wait. Call 119 right away.
