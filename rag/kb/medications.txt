Medication helper notes

- Keep medicine names short and consistent.
- Remind the user to follow the label or doctor instructions.
- If the user asks for dosing changes, say to confirm with a doctor or pharmacist.
- When possible, show timing in simple steps: morning, afternoon, evening, bedtime.
- For suspicious side effects, recommend urgent medical help.

Amlodipine (Blood Pressure Medicine)
- Take 1 tablet every morning with a full glass of water.
- Can be taken with or without food.
- Do not stop taking it without talking to your doctor first.
- Common side effects: swollen ankles, flushing, feeling tired.

Metformin (Diabetes Medicine)
- Take 1 tablet TWICE daily.
- Morning dose: take with breakfast.
- Evening dose: take with dinner.
- Always take with food to avoid stomach upset.
- Store at room temperature.

Atorvastatin (Cholesterol Medicine)
- Take 1 tablet every evening before bed.
- Can be taken with or without food.
- Avoid drinking grapefruit juice while taking this medicine.

Omeprazole (Stomach / Acid Medicine)
- Take 1 capsule 30 minutes BEFORE breakfast.
- Swallow the capsule whole — do not crush or chew it.
- Take it at the same time each day.

Aspirin (Heart / Blood Thinner)
- Take as directed by your doctor.
- Usually 1 small tablet daily with food.
- Tell your doctor if you notice unusual bruising or bleeding.

GENERAL RULES FOR ALL MEDICATIONS
- Never take double the dose if you miss one.
- Keep all medicines away from heat, sunlight, and moisture.
- Never share your medicines with others.
- Keep an up-to-date list of all your medicines for doctor visits.
